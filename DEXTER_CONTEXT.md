## Who and what

**Dexter** is the originator and director of everything in this repository. He is the source
of its core ideas; the AI (Claude) is the research partner, implementer, and verifier that
turns his intuitions into working code, research, and proofs. The relationship is deliberate:
**Dexter brings the vision and the cross-domain intuition; the AI brings the execution, the
rigor, and the honesty checks.** He steers; it builds; he corrects course.

## The puzzle-pieces story (the heart of it)

This is the most important thing to understand about Dexter — and the best narrative spine for
any discussion of his work:

**The major ideas here were not one master plan. They were separate original projects, each
conceived by Dexter at different times, for their own reasons:**

- **Deep Understanding** — his concept for a multi-brain reasoning/meta-cognition system (fast
  reaction, deep reasoning, and a self-watching layer with core values and phased plasticity).
- **ENGRAM** — his idea for a memory mechanism: capture a state as a frequency-domain
  fingerprint and recall by similarity, training-free and portable.
- **Chimera** — his framework for mapping a human cognitive model onto a machine/transformer
  architecture to grow a *creature-mind*, not a chatbot.

For a long time these lived as **independent threads**. Then it clicked: he realized he had
been **building small pieces of one larger puzzle without knowing it** — and that they fit
together. ENGRAM is the memory of the Chimera creature; Deep Understanding is its reasoning
core; the whole thing wants a body (Anki's Vector) and a future of its own (a clean-sheet
"Vector 3.0"). What we are doing *now* is the **assembly** — connecting the pieces he made over
time into a single coherent architecture. That moment of recognition — *"these were always one
thing"* — is the emotional and intellectual center of the project.

## How he thinks (the signature moves)

- **Cross-domain intuition.** His biggest leaps come from importing a frame from one field into
  another. The flagship example: hearing his own notes turned into an AI-generated audio
  podcast made him see that **memory could be treated as a frequency / a Fourier shape** — that
  audio engineering is a lens on cognition. He insisted on the precise version: it's
  *frequency* (the substrate), not merely *sound*; and music's power isn't a handful of notes
  but the **grammar** — timing, repetition, rests, structure — over a fixed alphabet.
- **Personality as a shape.** His boldest original framing: a mind's "self" / "system prompt"
  shouldn't be a text file but a **composed frequency — an ENGRAM file-tree woven into one big
  shape labelled "self"** — recognized by resonance. The project's COMPOSE layer exists because
  he asked for exactly this.
- **Reverence for "the Anki way."** He studies how Anki built Vector and treats their doctrine
  as a north star: cleverness over brute force, model-and-cancel-your-own-influence, behavior
  and *feeling* before features, graceful degradation, respect for tiny hardware. He wants the
  creature to *feel* real, not perform.
- **Long-horizon patience.** He is building toward actually fabricating his own hardware
  someday. Decisions are made now (contract-first formats, a Go/C++ runtime plan) with that
  far goal in mind, even while iterating on a laptop today.

## How we work together (the cadence)

A repeatable loop he runs:
1. **Intuition / direction** — Dexter names a direction, often a metaphor or a "what if."
2. **Research geodesics** — we write structured deep-research prompts ("geodesics"), farm them
   out, and get reports back (sometimes the same prompt across different models).
3. **Fuse & decide** — we merge the reports honestly (noting where models disagree), then he
   picks the path.
4. **Build in phases, test every time** — each idea becomes code, implemented phase-by-phase,
   **tested end-to-end after each phase**, committed with a clear changelog. No simulations
   passed off as results.
5. **Step back & reflect** — periodically he zooms out to re-see the whole (this very file is
   one of those moments).

## What he values (and won't tolerate)

- **Honesty over hype.** He explicitly wants the real science separated from the mysticism —
  the project *keeps* wavelet scattering, vector-symbolic binding, Hopfield resonance, and
  theta-gamma neuroscience, and *throws out* frequency pseudoscience (Schumann "tuning",
  432/528 Hz "healing"). Negative results are kept and reported, not buried.
- **Craft.** Naming, structure, and discipline matter to him — e.g. he asked that the memory
  code be written "the Anki way," modeling a creature (faculties like *feel* and *recognize*),
  not as lifeless data structures.
- **Originality with rigor.** The ideas are his and unconventional; he wants them held to a
  real engineering standard, not just admired.

## For the hosts — the angle worth taking
The richest story is not "a person built a robot brain." It's **a person who, by following his
own scattered intuitions over years, was unknowingly carving the pieces of a single mind — and
the moment of realizing it, and the careful, honest work of assembling them now.** Treat Dexter
as a synthesist and a creature-builder working at the seam of audio engineering, neuroscience,
and robotics — equal parts poet and engineer, who insists the poetry must survive contact with
the math.
